JC STORE

Comprehensive Enterprise Business Plan

Location: Phnom Penh, Cambodia | Sector: Retail

1. Executive Summary

Structured to dominate the localized retail sector by offering a hybrid product mix. This dual-pipeline approach ensures steady operational cash flow while allowing for capital accumulation during peak retail seasons. The integration of localized payment gateways (KHQR) and dynamic USD/KHR pricing ensures zero friction for consumers.

2. Market & Competitive Analysis

  • Target Demographic: Middle-to-upper income households seeking convenience and quality.
  • Competitive Advantage: Real-time inventory tracking, aggressive supplier negotiation on daily goods.
  • Economic Moat: Bespoke software management reduces overhead costs associated with shrinkage and stockouts by 40% compared to traditional local competitors.

3. Operational Strategy

Operations are driven by data. Every item is barcode-scanned upon entry, calculating real-time profit margins before hitting the shelf. Expiration tracking algorithms ensure perishable daily goods are moved via localized discounts before write-offs occur.

Key Operational Milestones
  • Month 1-3: System integration, supplier onboarding, layout optimization.
  • Month 4-6: Integration of KHQR, establishing localized e-commerce presence.
  • Month 7-12: Profit optimization, cutting lowest 10% performing SKUs.

4. Risk Mitigation Protocol

  • Supply Chain Disruption Maintain redundant local suppliers for high-velocity goods. Buffer stock of 15 days for imported non-perishables.
  • Currency Fluctuation Dual-currency pricing engine dynamically adjusts KHR tags based on daily peg, minimizing FX exposure.
  • Shrinkage & Theft Strict barcode-in/out policy tied to POS and daily cycle counts of top 20% value items.
